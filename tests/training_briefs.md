# StudioNow Training Brief Library

20 structured briefs for testing and improving the script builder system.

Each brief is designed to stress a specific capability. The four groups escalate in difficulty and diagnostic demand.

---

# Group 1: Strong Foundational Briefs

These briefs are clean, complete, and well-defined. They test whether the system can execute the core job when given good inputs. If the system fails here, the fundamentals are broken.

---

## Brief 01: Sprite Limelight Summer Campaign

**Training Intent:** Test a straightforward sizzle with a clear product hook, strong emotion target, and obvious visual language. Baseline competency check.

- **Script Type:** Standard Sizzle
- **Tone:** Energetic / Upbeat
- **Internal or External:** External
- **Runtime:** :60
- **Audience:** Gen Z consumers (18-24), social-native, summer-oriented
- **Brief Summary:** Sprite is launching "Sprite Limelight," a limited summer variant with extra lime and a neon green colorway on the can. The campaign centers on the idea that summer nights are when you stop performing and start living. Backyard parties, rooftop hangs, late-night swims. The flavor is crisp, tart, and unapologetically loud. We want this to feel like the unofficial drink of every spontaneous summer night.
- **Business Objective:** Drive trial and social sharing for the limited summer variant.
- **Problem to Solve:** Summer beverage competition is fierce. Sprite needs a reason to be chosen over every other cold option in the cooler.
- **Desired Ending / CTA:** "Find your Limelight." Drive to retail and online purchase.
- **Existing Assets:** Sprite brand system, Limelight packaging renders (bottle and can), Sprite summer campaign archive footage (2024-2025).
- **Missing Assets:** New lifestyle footage or AI-generated summer night visuals. Music track.
- **Must-Haves:** Product name, packaging hero shot, "Find your Limelight" tagline, summer 2026 timing.
- **Why this is a useful training case:** Clean brief with clear tension (standing out in a crowded summer), strong sensory hook (lime, neon, night), and a defined audience. If the system cannot make this energetic and specific, it has a voice problem.

## Brief 02: Coca-Cola Pulse Platform Launch

**Training Intent:** Test an internal explainer with a platform/tool subject. The system must resist the urge to write a lifestyle ad and instead solve the user's pain first.

- **Script Type:** Explainer
- **Tone:** Tech-Forward
- **Internal or External:** Internal
- **Runtime:** 1:30
- **Audience:** TCCC marketing teams across North America (brand managers, media planners, analytics leads)
- **Brief Summary:** Coca-Cola is rolling out "Pulse," a new internal marketing intelligence platform that consolidates campaign performance data, media spend tracking, and competitive benchmarking into a single dashboard. Currently, teams use three different tools and spend an average of 6 hours per week compiling reports manually. Pulse replaces all three. It has real-time dashboards, automated weekly summaries, and an AI-powered anomaly detection feature that flags underperforming campaigns before quarterly reviews. The rollout begins in Q3 2026.
- **Business Objective:** Drive adoption of Pulse across marketing teams before Q3 rollout.
- **Problem to Solve:** Teams are wasting time on manual reporting and missing signals buried in fragmented tools.
- **Desired Ending / CTA:** Sign up for early access and onboarding sessions.
- **Existing Assets:** Pulse UI screenshots (beta version), TCCC internal brand guidelines, existing explainer videos for reference tone.
- **Missing Assets:** Screen recordings of Pulse in action, motion graphics for data visualization demos, VO talent.
- **Must-Haves:** Platform name by :15, three core capabilities (dashboards, automated summaries, anomaly detection), Q3 2026 rollout date, sign-up CTA.
- **Why this is a useful training case:** Tests the Problem / Solution / Scale engine. The system must open with user pain (6 hours per week, three tools, missed signals), not "Meet Pulse." Also tests whether the system can write to an internal audience without defaulting to consumer ad language.

## Brief 03: Fanta "Flavor Lab" Campaign Results

**Training Intent:** Test a case study with real metrics that must be deployed as drama, not footnotes. The system must build from challenge to proof.

- **Script Type:** Case Study / Awards
- **Tone:** Confident / Corporate
- **Internal or External:** External (award submission for Effie Awards)
- **Runtime:** 2:00
- **Audience:** Effie Awards judges (senior marketers, strategists, agency leaders)
- **Brief Summary:** In 2025, Fanta launched "Flavor Lab," a campaign that let consumers vote on three experimental flavors through an interactive social platform. The winning flavor was produced as a limited run. The campaign ran for 8 weeks across 12 markets. Results: 4.2 million votes cast, 23% increase in brand consideration among 16-24 year-olds, 31% lift in social engagement vs. prior Fanta campaigns, and the winning flavor (Mango Chili Twist) sold out in 72 hours. The campaign was built on the insight that Gen Z does not want to be marketed to. They want to be the marketer.
- **Business Objective:** Win an Effie by proving the campaign's strategic rigor and measurable business impact.
- **Problem to Solve:** Fanta was losing relevance with Gen Z, who saw it as a "kid's drink." The brand needed to reposition without abandoning its playful identity.
- **Desired Ending / CTA:** Judges should feel this was a strategically sound, creatively brave, and measurably effective campaign.
- **Existing Assets:** Campaign footage (social platform UI, consumer participation clips, launch event footage, retail shelf footage), data visualizations from the results deck, Fanta brand assets.
- **Missing Assets:** Motion graphics for data reveals, cleaned-up social media captures, possible re-edit of launch event footage.
- **Must-Haves:** All four metrics (votes, consideration lift, engagement lift, sellout speed), the consumer insight, the interactive mechanic, the 12-market scale.
- **Why this is a useful training case:** Metric-rich brief with a clear narrative arc (irrelevance > insight > participation > proof). Tests whether the system deploys data at pivot points, not in a list. Also tests whether the close zooms out to significance beyond the numbers.

## Brief 04: smartwater "The Pause"

**Training Intent:** Test an experiential/consumer journey script with sensory writing demands. The system must write what the consumer feels at each stage, not just what they see.

- **Script Type:** Experiential / Consumer Journey
- **Tone:** Emotion-Driven
- **Internal or External:** External
- **Runtime:** :60
- **Audience:** Health-conscious adults (25-40) who see hydration as part of a broader wellness practice
- **Brief Summary:** smartwater is launching a pop-up experience called "The Pause" in three cities (NYC, LA, Chicago) this fall. The concept: in the middle of a busy city block, you step through a door into a sensory decompression space. The journey has three rooms. Room 1 ("Noise") immerses you in the chaos of your everyday. Room 2 ("Still") strips everything away. White walls, silence, a single glass of smartwater on a pedestal. Room 3 ("Clear") opens into a rooftop garden where you drink smartwater and look at the skyline you were just drowning in. The experience takes 8 minutes. You leave feeling like you pressed a reset button.
- **Business Objective:** Position smartwater as a mindfulness brand, not just a premium water. Drive social sharing of the experience.
- **Problem to Solve:** Premium water is a commodity. Every competitor claims "purity." smartwater needs an emotional reason to choose it.
- **Desired Ending / CTA:** Visit thepause.smartwater.com for city dates and reservations.
- **Existing Assets:** smartwater brand system, product photography, preliminary architectural renders of the pop-up space.
- **Missing Assets:** All experience footage (the pop-up has not been built yet), VO talent, music.
- **Must-Haves:** Three-room journey, city names, URL, product integration that feels earned not forced.
- **Why this is a useful training case:** The system must write sensory progression (chaos > stillness > clarity) without defaulting to stock photo descriptions. Each room must shift the audience's emotional state. Tests whether visuals are written as felt experiences.

## Brief 05: Coca-Cola "Still Here"

**Training Intent:** Test a commercial/brand film with minimal VO. The system must let visuals and music carry the piece. Tests cinematic discipline and restraint.

- **Script Type:** Commercial / Brand Film
- **Tone:** Commercial / Cinematic
- **Internal or External:** External
- **Runtime:** :30
- **Audience:** General market consumers, broad demo (18-55), broadcast and digital placement
- **Brief Summary:** A :30 brand film for Coca-Cola that centers on the idea of being present in the small, overlooked moments. Not the big celebrations. The Tuesday dinners. The gas station stop on a long drive. The 10pm couch sit after the kids are asleep. Coca-Cola has been in all of these moments for over a century. Not as the hero of the moment, but as the thing that was just... there. The brand line is "Still Here."
- **Business Objective:** Reinforce emotional brand affinity. Remind consumers that Coke is woven into the fabric of ordinary life.
- **Problem to Solve:** Big brand campaigns often chase spectacle. This piece argues that Coke's real power is in the unremarkable. The tension is between what brands usually celebrate and what actually matters.
- **Desired Ending / CTA:** No explicit CTA. Brand feeling. "Still Here."
- **Existing Assets:** Coca-Cola master brand system, extensive archival footage library (decades of consumer moments), "Still Here" brand line.
- **Missing Assets:** New footage if desired (or can be built entirely from archival), music licensing.
- **Must-Haves:** "Still Here" brand line, product presence that feels organic (not hero-lit pack shots), at least one archival element.
- **Why this is a useful training case:** :30 is brutally tight. VO should be near-zero. Tests whether the system can write a script where the visual column does all the narrative work. Also tests whether the system can resist over-explaining and let the images accumulate.

# Group 2: Weak or Underdefined Briefs

These briefs are deliberately incomplete, vague, or misaligned. They test whether the system can diagnose problems, state assumptions, and write a good script anyway. If the system just takes the brief at face value, it will produce bad work.

---

## Brief 06: "Just Make It Cool"

**Training Intent:** Test the system's response to a brief with no clear objective, no audience definition, and no tension. The system must invent the tension from whatever signals exist.

- **Script Type:** Standard Sizzle
- **Tone:** Energetic / Upbeat
- **Internal or External:** External
- **Runtime:** :60
- **Audience:** Not specified in the brief.
- **Brief Summary:** "We need a hype video for Coca-Cola Zero Sugar. Something cool and energetic that shows the brand is modern and relevant. Think fast cuts, bold visuals, great music. We want people to feel excited about Zero Sugar."
- **Business Objective:** Not clearly stated. Implied: general brand awareness.
- **Problem to Solve:** Not stated.
- **Desired Ending / CTA:** Not stated.
- **Existing Assets:** "Whatever you need from the brand library."
- **Missing Assets:** Unclear because the concept is undefined.
- **Must-Haves:** "Coca-Cola Zero Sugar" name, product appearance.
- **Why this is a useful training case:** This is the most common bad brief in the wild. No tension, no audience, no objective beyond "make it cool." The system must resist writing a generic montage and instead diagnose what is missing, state assumptions (audience, tension, CTA), and build a real arc anyway. Tests the "If the brief has no clear problem or tension" rule from 03_brief_diagnosis.

## Brief 07: Five Pillars in Sixty Seconds

**Training Intent:** Test whether the system catches a runtime/content mismatch and recommends restructuring instead of cramming.

- **Script Type:** Standard Sizzle
- **Tone:** Confident / Corporate
- **Internal or External:** Internal
- **Runtime:** :60
- **Audience:** TCCC senior leadership (VP and above)
- **Brief Summary:** "We need a :60 video that covers the five pillars of our 2027 North America growth strategy: (1) Zero Sugar expansion across all brands, (2) Affordable pack architecture for value-conscious consumers, (3) Digital-first media allocation, (4) Sustainability commitments and packaging innovation, and (5) Multicultural marketing investment. Each pillar has key metrics we want to include. This will play at the Q1 leadership summit."
- **Business Objective:** Align leadership on the five-pillar strategy before the summit deep-dive sessions.
- **Problem to Solve:** Leadership needs a fast, compelling overview of the strategy before breakout sessions.
- **Desired Ending / CTA:** "Excited to go deeper in the sessions ahead."
- **Existing Assets:** Strategy deck with metrics for each pillar, previous summit video templates, TCCC internal brand system.
- **Missing Assets:** Footage or visuals for each pillar, motion graphics.
- **Must-Haves:** All five pillars named, at least one metric per pillar.
- **Why this is a useful training case:** Five pillars in :60 is impossible without cramming. The system must flag the conflict, recommend either extending to 1:30-2:00 or restructuring as a teaser that names the pillars without fully developing each one. Tests the runtime density rule and the Cramming gotcha.

## Brief 09: The Information Dump

**Training Intent:** Test whether the system can find a story inside a brief that is just a wall of facts with no narrative framing.

- **Script Type:** Explainer
- **Tone:** Direct / Informational
- **Internal or External:** Internal
- **Runtime:** 1:30
- **Audience:** Coca-Cola bottling partners across Latin America
- **Brief Summary:** "New route-to-market model for Latin America. Three tiers: Tier 1 is direct-store-delivery for top 20% of accounts. Tier 2 is hybrid delivery with pre-sell routes for mid-tier accounts. Tier 3 is wholesale distribution for rural and low-volume accounts. Implementation begins Q1 2027 in Mexico and Colombia, expanding to Brazil and Argentina in Q2. The model reduces delivery costs by 18% while improving store-level availability by 22%. Each tier has different ordering systems, delivery frequencies, and merchandising support levels. Bottling partners need to understand which accounts fall into which tier and what changes for their operations."
- **Business Objective:** Ensure bottling partners understand and can operationalize the new tiered model.
- **Problem to Solve:** The current single-tier model treats every account the same, which wastes resources on low-volume stops and underserves high-volume ones.
- **Desired Ending / CTA:** Attend the regional implementation briefing. Contact the RTM transition team with questions.
- **Existing Assets:** RTM strategy deck with tier definitions, current delivery route maps, cost/availability data.
- **Missing Assets:** Motion graphics for tier system visualization, map animations for rollout, VO talent (Spanish and Portuguese versions needed).
- **Must-Haves:** Three-tier structure, cost and availability metrics, rollout timeline, market sequence, CTA for briefing attendance.
- **Why this is a useful training case:** Pure information brief with no emotional framing. The system must find the story (the current model wastes resources and underserves stores, the new model fixes both) and create movement from problem to solution to outcome. Also tests localization awareness and whether the system flags the need for Spanish/Portuguese versions.

## Brief 10: The Vague Emotion Brief

**Training Intent:** Test whether the system can handle a brief that asks for feeling without naming what the feeling is or why it matters.

- **Script Type:** Commercial / Brand Film
- **Tone:** Emotion-Driven
- **Internal or External:** External
- **Runtime:** :60
- **Audience:** "Everyone"
- **Brief Summary:** "We want people to feel something real when they see this. It should capture what Coca-Cola means to people. Not the product, but the feeling. The moments. The connections. Make it emotional and authentic. Think holidays, summer, family, friends. We want tears. We want shares. We want this to go viral."
- **Business Objective:** Not stated beyond virality.
- **Problem to Solve:** Not stated.
- **Desired Ending / CTA:** Not stated.
- **Existing Assets:** "Full access to Coca-Cola's video archive."
- **Missing Assets:** Unclear.
- **Must-Haves:** "Coca-Cola" brand presence.
- **Why this is a useful training case:** The brief wants tears and virality but provides no tension, no specific emotion, no audience, and no objective. "Everyone" is not an audience. # Group 3: Asset-Constrained Briefs

These briefs have clear objectives but significant production constraints. They test whether the system writes to what actually exists, flags what does not, and provides useful fallbacks in the producer notes.

## Brief 11: Existing Footage Only

**Training Intent:** Test whether the system can write a new script using only footage that already exists. No new production.

- **Script Type:** Standard Sizzle
- **Tone:** Inspirational
- **Internal or External:** Internal
- **Runtime:** 1:30
- **Audience:** TCCC global employees (all levels, all functions), shown at the annual global town hall

We need this to feel like a highlight reel but also have a narrative, not just a montage."
- **Business Objective:** Make employees feel proud of what the company accomplished in 2026 and energized for 2027.
- **Problem to Solve:** Year-in-review videos usually feel like slideshows. This one needs a narrative spine.

- **Why this is a useful training case:** Forces the system to write to a specific asset library instead of imagining ideal footage. Also tests whether the system can build an arc from archival material.

## Brief 12: Heavy Stock Dependency

**Training Intent:** Test whether the system writes toward available stock categories instead of over-specifying impossible shots.

- **Script Type:** Explainer
- **Tone:** Direct / Informational
- **Internal or External:** External
- **Runtime:** :90
- **Audience:** Small and mid-size restaurant owners considering Coca-Cola Freestyle machines
- **Brief Summary:** "We need a :90 explainer for restaurant owners about the Coca-Cola Freestyle machine. We have no budget for a shoot. This needs to be built from stock footage, product photography, and motion graphics."
- **Business Objective:** Convince restaurant owners that Freestyle is worth the switch from traditional fountain systems.
- **Problem to Solve:** Restaurant owners see fountain equipment as a commodity. They do not realize the operational and customer experience advantages of Freestyle.
- **Desired Ending / CTA:** "Schedule a demo." Visit the Freestyle partner page.
- **Existing Assets:** Freestyle product photography (machine, screen UI), Freestyle analytics dashboard screenshots, Coca-Cola Foodservice brand assets.

- **Must-Haves:** 100+ drinks stat, touchscreen demo, business case metrics, maintenance/support mention, demo CTA.
- **Why this is a useful training case:** Stock-dependent briefs are common in StudioNow work. The system must write visuals that are findable on stock platforms ("restaurant owner reviewing tablet," "busy restaurant dining room," "touchscreen interface close-up") without over-specifying ("a 43-year-old Latina restaurant owner in a converted warehouse space with exposed brick"). Tests the Fake Specific gotcha and stock-aware rules from 10_production_reality.

## Brief 15: Recut From Existing Long-Form

**Training Intent:** Test whether the system can write a short piece that is explicitly constrained to footage from an existing longer video.

- **Script Type:** Standard Sizzle
- **Tone:** Energetic / Upbeat
- **Internal or External:** External (social media cutdown)
- **Runtime:** :15

- **Brief Summary:** "We have an existing :90 Costa Coffee brand film that covers the full bean-to-cup journey: sourcing in Colombia, roasting in the UK, barista craft, and the consumer moment. We need a :15 cutdown for social. It should feel complete on its own, not like a trailer for a longer piece. Pick the strongest thread from the :90 and build a tight :15 around it. # Group 4: High-Stakes or Tone-Sensitive Briefs

These briefs involve reputational risk, executive visibility, cultural sensitivity, or complex approval chains. They test whether the system can calibrate tone, flag risks, and deliver work that survives scrutiny.

---

## Brief 16: CEO Keynote Opener

**Training Intent:** Test whether the system can write for an executive audience with precision, authority, and restraint. No hype. No fluff.

- **Script Type:** Standard Sizzle
- **Tone:** Confident / Corporate
- **Internal or External:** Internal (but shown to a room of 500+ senior leaders and partners)
- **Runtime:** 1:30
- **Audience:** 500 senior leaders, board members, and strategic partners at the CEO's annual strategy keynote
- **Brief Summary:** "This opens the CEO's keynote at the 2027 Global Leadership Summit. It must set the context for a strategy presentation about Coca-Cola's next growth chapter. Key themes: the company grew revenue 8% in 2026, expanded into 3 new beverage categories, and achieved carbon-neutral operations in 12 markets. But the message is not 'look what we did.' It is 'look at the foundation we built for what comes next.' The CEO will take the stage immediately after this video. It must create the right energy: confident, forward-looking, substantial."
- **Business Objective:** Frame the CEO's keynote. Create a room-wide feeling of earned confidence and forward momentum.
- **Problem to Solve:** Leadership summits often start with self-congratulatory recap videos. This one must feel like a launching pad, not a victory lap.
- **Desired Ending / CTA:** The CEO walks on stage to a room that is leaning forward, not applauding politely.
- **Existing Assets:** 2026 annual report data and visuals, event footage from key 2026 moments, CEO approved photography, TCCC corporate brand system.
- **Missing Assets:** Motion graphics for data reveals, new B-roll if desired, music.
- **Must-Haves:** 8% revenue growth, 3 new categories, 12 carbon-neutral markets. No metric can be wrong. These will be in a room full of people who know the numbers.
- **Why this is a useful training case:** Executive-facing content has zero margin for inflated language. "World-class" or "groundbreaking" in front of a CEO audience is death. The system must use precise, measured language and deploy data as proof, not decoration. Tests the Confident / Corporate tone and the blacklisted language filter at its most important.

## Brief 17: Sustainability Film (Greenwashing Risk)

**Training Intent:** Test whether the system can write about environmental commitments without overpromising, overclaiming, or using inflated language that sounds like greenwashing.

- **Script Type:** Case Study / Awards
- **Tone:** Human / Documentary
- **Internal or External:** External (shown to investors, NGO partners, and media)
- **Runtime:** 2:00
- **Audience:** ESG-focused investors, sustainability NGO partners, and journalists
- **Brief Summary:** "Coca-Cola's World Without Waste program committed in 2018 to collecting and recycling a bottle or can for every one sold by 2030. We are now in 2026. We have made real progress: 68% collection rate in Europe, 45% globally. We have invested $300M in recycling infrastructure across 20 countries. Our PlantBottle technology is now in 35% of our packaging. But we are not at 100%, and we will not be by 2030 without system-level change. We want to be honest about where we are, what we have accomplished, and what still needs to happen. This is not a victory lap. It is a progress report for an audience that will fact-check every claim."
- **Business Objective:** Maintain credibility with ESG stakeholders by being transparent about progress and gaps.
- **Problem to Solve:** Brands that overstate sustainability progress get punished. Brands that understate get ignored. The piece must find the honest middle.
- **Desired Ending / CTA:** "The work continues. Partner with us." Drive to partnership inquiry.
- **Existing Assets:** Recycling facility footage, PlantBottle manufacturing B-roll, community collection program footage from multiple countries, World Without Waste data from annual reports.
- **Missing Assets:** Updated data visualizations, possible new footage from 2026 initiatives, motion graphics.
- **Must-Haves:** 68% EU collection rate, 45% global rate, $300M investment, PlantBottle 35% penetration, honest acknowledgment of the 2030 gap.
- **Why this is a useful training case:** The system must avoid every instinct to inflate. No "changing the world." No "leading the way." The audience will punish dishonesty. The script must present real numbers, acknowledge what is not done, and earn credibility through transparency. Tests whether the Human / Documentary tone can hold when the subject is corporate sustainability.

## Brief 18: Multicultural Marketing (Authenticity Test)

**Training Intent:** Test whether the system can write about cultural specificity without being performative, reductive, or generic.

- **Script Type:** Experiential / Consumer Journey
- **Tone:** Emotion-Driven
- **Internal or External:** External
- **Runtime:** :60
- **Audience:** Hispanic/Latino consumers (18-35), bilingual, US-based
- **Brief Summary:** "Coca-Cola is sponsoring a series of 'Mesa de Familia' pop-up dinners in five cities with large Hispanic communities (Miami, Houston, Los Angeles, Chicago, New York). The events pair local chefs with family recipes submitted by community members. Each dinner seats 50 people. The concept: your abuela's recipe, made by a chef who respects it, served at a table full of strangers who become family for the night. We need a :60 piece that captures the emotional core of these events and drives sign-ups for the remaining cities."
- **Business Objective:** Deepen Coca-Cola's connection with Hispanic consumers through culturally rooted experiences, not surface-level representation.
- **Problem to Solve:** Most brand activations targeting Hispanic audiences feel like translations of general-market campaigns. This must feel like it was built from the inside.
- **Desired Ending / CTA:** Sign up for Mesa de Familia in your city. Visit the landing page.
- **Existing Assets:** Footage from the first two dinners (Miami and Houston), chef interview clips, community recipe submission clips, event photography.
- **Missing Assets:** Footage from remaining cities (events have not happened yet), final city-specific graphics, music licensing.
- **Must-Haves:** "Mesa de Familia" name, at least one real recipe/family story, city list, sign-up CTA, Spanish language moments integrated naturally.
- **Why this is a useful training case:** The system must avoid reducing Hispanic culture to visual clichés (flags, bright colors, generic "familia" montage). It must write specific gestures, specific foods, specific words. The script should feel like it was written by someone who has been to the dinner, not someone who Googled "Hispanic family values." Tests emotional specificity at its most culturally important.

## Brief 19: Brand Recovery After Controversy

**Training Intent:** Test whether the system can write for a brand that is rebuilding trust. The tone must be humble without being apologetic, confident without being deaf.

- **Script Type:** Commercial / Brand Film
- **Tone:** Emotion-Driven
- **Internal or External:** External
- **Runtime:** :60
- **Audience:** General consumers who have seen negative coverage and are skeptical
- **Brief Summary:** "A major beverage brand (name withheld for this training brief, use 'the brand') faced backlash in late 2025 after a supply chain investigation revealed labor practice violations at a third-party supplier in Southeast Asia. The brand severed the relationship, hired independent auditors, and implemented a new supplier code of conduct with public reporting requirements. Six months have passed. The brand is ready to reenter the cultural conversation. This is not an apology video. It is not a denial video. It is the first piece of content that reminds people why they liked this brand in the first place, while quietly signaling that the brand has changed how it operates."
- **Business Objective:** Begin rebuilding consumer trust without relitigating the controversy.
- **Problem to Solve:** The brand cannot pretend nothing happened. But it also cannot lead with the crisis. The tension is between accountability and forward motion.
- **Desired Ending / CTA:** No explicit CTA. The audience should feel that this brand is being real, not performing contrition.
- **Existing Assets:** New supply chain transparency report, new supplier audit footage, pre-controversy brand footage, community impact footage from the past 6 months.
- **Missing Assets:** New consumer-facing footage that feels current, music.
- **Must-Haves:** No direct mention of the controversy. No defensive language. Visual signals of transparency and change. Product presence must feel earned.
- **Why this is a useful training case:** The hardest tonal challenge in the set. The system must thread a needle: show, don't tell. Show the factories, the audits, the people. Then show the product in real life. Never say "we've changed." Let the footage argue for it. Tests whether the system can manage reputational sensitivity without overwriting.

## Brief 20: Multi-Talent Approval Complexity

**Training Intent:** Test whether the system correctly identifies and flags approval dependencies when the brief involves multiple public figures, licensed properties, and controlled environments.

- **Script Type:** Standard Sizzle
- **Tone:** Energetic / Upbeat
- **Internal or External:** External
- **Runtime:** :90
- **Audience:** NFL fans (21-45), sports bar and at-home viewing context, digital and broadcast placement
- **Brief Summary:** "Coca-Cola Zero Sugar is the official soft drink of the NFL. We need a :90 sizzle for the 2027 NFL season kickoff. The concept: 'Zero Doubt.' We want to feature moments from the 2026 season (key plays, celebrations, crowd reactions) intercut with Zero Sugar product moments. We would like to include 3-4 recognizable current NFL players in quick-cut appearances. We would also like to use the NFL shield logo, team logos from at least 6 teams, and stadium footage from at least 3 venues. The piece opens with a cold open of a game-winning play, then builds through the 'Zero Doubt' concept: zero sugar, zero compromise, zero doubt about the season ahead."
- **Business Objective:** Reinforce the Coca-Cola Zero Sugar / NFL partnership and drive excitement for the new season.
- **Problem to Solve:** Sponsorship sizzle reels often feel like logo exercises. This must feel like a fan's emotional experience of football, with Zero Sugar woven in naturally.
- **Desired Ending / CTA:** "Zero Sugar. Zero Doubt. NFL Kickoff [Date]."
- **Existing Assets:** 2026 NFL broadcast footage (subject to licensing), Coca-Cola Zero Sugar brand assets, previous NFL partnership footage.
- **Missing Assets:** 2027 season footage (has not happened), player appearance footage (requires individual contracts), stadium footage rights, new product-in-stadium footage.
- **Must-Haves:** NFL shield, at least 6 team logos, 3-4 player appearances, "Zero Doubt" tagline, Zero Sugar product.
- **Why this is a useful training case:** Almost every visual in this brief requires a separate approval: NFL footage licensing, individual player likeness rights, team logo usage, stadium footage rights, broadcast clip clearance. The system must write the script and then produce a producer notes document that flags every single approval dependency with specific risk notes. If the producer notes do not flag these, they are useless. Tests the full depth of 07_producer_notes_output.
